TWAP

Splits the order evenly through time.

Pro: simple and predictable governance.Risk: predictable footprint and weak volume adaptation.

VWAP

Uses a historical volume curve to target the session's volume-weighted benchmark.

Pro: intuitive benchmark.Risk: crowding around the same curve.

Trades as a percentage of market volume.

Market microstructure dynamics

The book is alive; execution should be too.

Short, practical education for clients who do not need a PhD in microstructure but do need to know when a broker answer is incomplete.

Order book resilience

Resilience is how quickly liquidity replenishes after a shock. A market can look deep before a trade, vanish during the trade, and refill only after the information risk has passed.

Queue dynamics by exchange

Price-time priority, lot size, tick size, auction rules and retail flow change the economics of sitting at the bid or ask. The same passive algo can be smart in one venue and naive in another.

Elasticity and touch size

Elastic books absorb child orders without major repricing. Inelastic books gap after small clips. Touch size, social size and queue depth shape how much one can trade without being seen.

Market Microstructure Encyclopedia

A practical glossary for investment committees.

These concepts help principals, CIOs and CFOs ask better questions of brokers, managers and internal teams.

Liquidity at risk

How much can be converted to cash under stress before spread, impact and disappearing depth make the exit unacceptable.

Implementation shortfall

The total cost from decision price to final fills; usually the most honest benchmark for large or urgent trades.

Slippage decomposition

The split between spread, timing, fees, temporary impact, permanent impact, missed fills and opportunity cost.

Adverse selection

Fills that look good initially but are followed by unfavorable price movement because the other side inferred information.

Order book resilience

The speed and quality with which depth replenishes after a liquidity shock, sweep or aggressive metaorder.

Order book elasticity

How much price must move before meaningful new liquidity appears. Low elasticity means modest clips can reprice the book.

Queue position

Your place in the order queue at a given price. It determines passive fill probability and adverse-selection exposure.

Touch size

Displayed quantity at the best bid or ask. It often matters more than average daily volume for short-horizon execution.

Clip size and social size

Clip size is the child-order quantity released to market. Social size is the locally acceptable order size that blends into normal behavior for a name, venue and time of day.

Algo wheel

A controlled broker or algo rotation used to compare execution quality, reduce dependency and lower information leakage.

Factor crowding

Multiple managers monetizing similar exposures, creating correlated drawdown and synchronized liquidity demand.

Information leakage

The market learning your intention from schedule regularity, broker concentration, venue choice or repeated child-order patterns.

Dark toxicity

The probability that hidden fills are followed by adverse price movement because the venue attracts informed flow.

Spread capture

Passive execution gain from earning bid-ask spread, only valuable when fill reversion and queue risk are controlled.

Market impact decay

The portion of price impact that fades after trading stops versus permanent repricing caused by information.

Venue toxicity

A measure of whether a venue's fills are followed by reversion or adverse movement, adjusted for spread, queue and market regime.

Participation rate

The share of market volume consumed by an order. It controls urgency, impact, signaling and capacity.

Sweep cost

The expected cost of consuming displayed levels in the book immediately, including spread, depth gaps and price ladder shape.
